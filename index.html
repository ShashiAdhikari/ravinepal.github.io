Ravi Kumar, @RaviNepal

Leading Digital Strategy for Governance Global Practice at the World Bank

Ravi is an intrapreneur, digital strategy leader and good governance advocate. He believes technology and media have the power to create opportunities and has worked to make that happen in his home country of Nepal.

He leads digital strategy for Governance Global Practice at the World Bank in Washington, D.C. At night, he works on his latest start up, Code for Nepal, to increase digital literacy and use of open data in Nepal. He also serves on Buena Vista University’s President’s Advisory Council, and is a Penn Kemble Fellow at the National Endowment for Democracy.

In his previous role at the World Bank, he managed YouThink!, the World Bank’s online space for youth, in six languages. He has also published on the World Economic Forum, The Huffington Post, Global Post & national newspapers in Nepal.

In November 2013, he was a visiting Digital Media lecturer and Academic and Cultural Series Speaker at Buena Vista University, his alma mater, where he earned his BA.

In 2009, he co-founded Grassroot Movement in Nepal, a non-profit that has rebuilt more than 25 schools.

He has a MS degree from Columbia University where he specialized in digital media and was a Fellow at Toni Stabile Center.
